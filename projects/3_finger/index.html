Biometrics Anti‑Spoofing System for Galaxy S Series

On‑device fingerprint anti‑spoofing system designed for Galaxy devices to detect fake biometric inputs and ensure secure user authentication.

##

From September 2019 to September 2022, Sung‑Un Park designed the architecture of Samsung’s on‑device fingerprint anti‑spoofing system deployed in Galaxy smartphones. The project focused on developing robust algorithms that detect fake fingerprints made from materials such as silicone or gel while maintaining low latency on embedded processors. The resulting system adapts to diverse user conditions and environmental variations and was shipped on multiple Galaxy S series devices cv.stoz.kr .

Contributions

Architecture design: Designed the on‑device anti‑spoofing software stack, integrating it with the fingerprint sensor and secure enclave.

Spoof‑detection algorithms: Developed machine‑learning models that analyse fingerprint ridge patterns and material characteristics to discriminate between live and fake fingerprints cv.stoz.kr .

Adaptive thresholds: Implemented adaptation schemes that adjust detection thresholds based on user feedback and sensor conditions to reduce false rejections and improve robustness.
